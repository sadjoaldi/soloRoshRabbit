About Rabbit

The rabbit is a small mammal that belongs to the family Leporidae, which also includes hares. There are about 30 species of rabbits around the world. Rabbits are herbivores, which means they only eat plants. They are known for their long ears, short tails, and powerful hind legs. Rabbits are social animals that live in groups called colonies. They are also known for their ability to reproduce quickly. Rabbits are found in a variety of habitats, including forests, grasslands, deserts, and wetlands.
